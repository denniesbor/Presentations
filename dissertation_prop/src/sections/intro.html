Policy Context

National Space Weather Strategy mandates quantitative infrastructure assessment

Whole-of-government approach to prepare, forecast, characterize, recover (e.g., NOAA SWPC, USGS Geomagnetism Program)

2015 SWORM 6 goals: benchmarks, response, mitigation,
assessment, forecasting, cooperation
2019 Natl SW Strategy 3 pillars: asset protection,
forecasting, coordinated response
2020 PROSWIFT NOAA operational mandate
2023 Plan 5-year roadmap

Yet...

No quantitative framework exists to assess space weather as a risk

Example Carrington-level event: "trillions in damages"
But no physics-based foundation, no uncertainty quantification, no cross-hazard context

Problem Statement

Disciplinary Silos

Physics, engineering, economics work separately

No UQ

Scenario-based estimates without uncertainty propagation

No Comparison Framework

Cannot compare to hurricanes, floods, earthquakes

Physics Study space environment
Engineering Study infrastructure
Economics Estimate losses
Nobody connects them with rigorous UQ

Research Question 1

Physical Vulnerability

How does extreme space weather stress power grids and orbital assets, with quantified uncertainty?

Focus Physical stress + UQ
Grid GIC -> transformer overheating risk
Space Drag/radiation -> service loss
UQ Topology, grounding, density models

Research Question 2

Socio-Economic Consequence

How do physical stresses translate to service disruption, affected population, and daily economic loss?

Chain Stress -> service -> IO/CGE -> loss
Outputs Exceedance curves,
return levels, credible intervals
Economy Sectoral loss and daily GDP
Note Full distributions,
not point estimates
