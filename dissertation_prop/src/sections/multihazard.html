Publication III: Multi-Hazard Comparative Assessment

Proposed Integration

Use NSF–NCAR continental, granular wind/hurricane hazard layers within the existing physics-to-economics framework for power, roads, bridges, and communications.

Comparison Goal

Place space weather alongside hurricanes using the same metrics, units, and uncertainty treatment without changing downstream pipeline.

Layers NSF–NCAR wind/hurricane
Assets Power, roads, bridges, comms
Metrics Exceedance, return levels, intervals
Scope Continental, granular

Research Questions

RQ1

How to harmonize NSF–NCAR wind/hurricane layers with the current framework while preserving hazard physics and uncertainty quantification?

RQ2

How does space weather compare to hurricanes across power and transport networks using the same risk metrics and uncertainty bands?

Examples 100-year levels; exceedance curves
Assets Substations; primary roads
Outputs Daily GDP intervals; affected users
UQ Propagated end to end

Expected Results: Comparative Risk Metrics

Economic Scale

Daily economic impact by hazard.

Example: 100-year space weather ≈ \$1.4B/day; Cat 3 hurricane ≈ \$0.9–2.1B/day; major earthquake ≈ \$3.5–4.0B/day.

Spatial Distribution

Where impacts concentrate.

Example: Space weather: high latitudes; hurricanes: coasts; earthquakes: fault zones.

Sectoral Vulnerability

Most affected sectors.

Example: Space weather: power (5–40%); floods/earthquakes: transport (15–35%).

Temporal Profile

Duration of disruption.

Example: Space weather: 12–72 hours; hurricanes: 2–4 weeks; earthquakes: 1–6 months.

Contributions

Key Contributions

  1. Unified multi-hazard framework: Integrated method bridging space weather and terrestrial hazards with preserved physics.
  2. Quantitative contextualization: Positions space weather within the natural hazard landscape.
  3. Interdependency modeling: Captures compound events and cascading failures.
  4. National comparative metrics: Apples-to-apples results with consistent uncertainty.
