Research Question 1

Physical Vulnerability

How does extreme space weather stress power grids and orbital assets, with quantified uncertainty?

Focus Physical stress + UQ
Grid GIC -> transformer overheating risk
Space Drag/radiation -> service loss

Research Question 2

Socio-Economic Consequence

How do physical stresses translate to service disruption, affected population, and daily economic loss?

Chain Stress -> service -> IO/CGE -> loss
Outputs Exceedance curves, return levels, credible intervals
Economy Sectoral loss and daily GDP
Note Full distributions, not point estimates

Research Question 3

Comparative Risk Assessment

How does space weather compare to hurricanes, floods, and earthquakes on the same quantitative scale?

Approach Physics to economics pipeline
Models NSF-NCAR high resolution; add road networks and other infrastructure
Context Builds on existing multi-hazard frameworks
Goal National apples-to-apples with credible intervals

Research Objectives

Objective 1

Quantify component stress and failure probabilities with explicit uncertainty propagation.

Objective 2

Translate degradation into service loss and probabilistic nationwide economic impacts.

Objective 3

Compare hazards on a common scale for planning and prioritization.

Objective 4

Integrate granular NSF-NCAR models within multi-hazard infrastructure assessment frameworks nationally.

Metrics GIC, thermal load, decay, delta v, dose, SEE
Chain Stress to service to IO or CGE to GDP
Context Multi hazard baseline, apples to apples
UQ Propagated across all stages
