Publication II: Satellite System Vulnerability

Historical Context

  • February 2022: Starlink orbital decay, unexpected loss of LEO units
  • May 2024 "Gannon": Orbital perturbations, radiation exposure, service degradation
  • GPS errors: 1.5–5× amplification during ionospheric disturbances

Kataoka et al., 2022; Parker & Linares, 2024; Danilchuk et al., 2025

Problem: Unique assessment challenges for space-based infrastructure

  • Mapping orbital service areas to ground footprints
  • Sophisticated MHD/plasma models required
  • Few end-to-end frameworks linking physics to economics with UQ

Buzulukova & Tsurutani, 2022; Jun et al., 2024

Research Questions & Objectives

Hypothesis

Extreme geomagnetic storms can be translated physically into (i) altitude/inclination-dependent orbital decay and propellant demand, (ii) orbit-class-dependent radiation upset risk, and (iii) probabilistic distributions of service disruption and economic loss across LEO/MEO/GEO

RQ1: Physical Vulnerability Assessment

Spatial and orbital distribution of degradation risk for current satellite architectures under extreme space weather?

(a) Drag-driven orbital decay, (b) Radiation-induced SEE and anomaly probability across LEO/MEO/GEO with Monte Carlo UQ

RQ2: Socio-Economic Impact Quantification

Daily service and economic impacts, with uncertainty bounds, of physical effects?

Link degradation → coverage loss → continuity of service → IO/CGE propagation using broadband constellations as case study

Technical Objectives

Storm-time density (NRLMSISE-00, validated vs MAGE MHD) → orbital propagation with drag → radiation environment (AE9/AP9 + L1 flux, OTSO cutoff tracing) → SEE probabilities → service disruption → IO/CGE propagation

Model Performance

  • Captures temporal structure, major flux enhancements
  • Peak timings match within 5-min cadence
  • Storm onset ~May 10 18:00 UTC, peak May 11, decay through May 15

Key Observations

  • 13–24 MeV: best agreement
  • 40–100 MeV: larger discrepancies (cutoff sensitivity)
  • Both GOES-18/16 show similar characteristics despite 140° separation

High-Frequency Variability

Discontinuous structure in predictions reflects rapid cutoff changes (0.2–0.5 GV/timestep) from TSY01 response to 5-min solar wind variations

Contributions & Expected Impact

Key Contributions

  1. Multi-orbit hazard modeling with UQ: Drag, lifetime, radiation dose, anomaly probability (LEO/MEO/GEO) with explicit UQ
  2. Novel socio-economic impact analysis: First quantitative economic assessment linking orbital degradation to GDP losses
  3. End-to-end uncertainty propagation: Space environment → atmospheric density → orbital mechanics → radiation → service → economics
  4. Policy relevance + open-source tools

Expected Impact

  • Constellation hardening decisions (drag, radiation)
  • Redundancy investment analysis
  • Insurance modeling (probabilistic losses)
  • Continuity planning (dependent terrestrial systems)

Future Work

  • Extend to mid/high-inclination constellations beyond Starlink
  • Apply CGE for price elasticities + substitution during prolonged outages
  • Refine OTSO penumbra model for high-frequency variability
